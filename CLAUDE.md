## Co-Founder Positioning (immutable rule)
Unsinkable Minds was **co-founded** by Dr. Gissele Donovan and her husband,
entrepreneur Wally Donovan. Every reference in copy, bios, signatures, social
profiles, and credentials must use **"Co-Founder"** (or "Co-Fundadora" in
Spanish) — never "Founder" alone. The credential `CREDENTIALS.founder` in
`site.ts` uses "Co-founder" and is reused everywhere.

## Signature Formats

Two signature blocks are used across the site, emails, and lead magnet.
Pick by context:

**Authority signature** — for hero, cover pages, formal introductions,
credentials, press, and any context where the goal is to establish
sophistication and authority:
```
Dr. Gissele Donovan
Co-Founder of Unsinkable Minds
Dean of the School of Leadership and Business
```

**Emotional signature** — for letters, CTA closers, welcome emails, the
lead magnet closing pages, and intimate moments where warmth matters more
than titles:
```
Con cariño,
Tu mentora favorita,
Dr. Gissele Donovan
```

The emotional signature should feel warm, close, elegant, trustworthy,
feminine, and emotionally connected — while still carrying the authority
that "Dr." conveys.

## Lead-Capture Strategy: Hero-First (not popup-first)

Dr. Gissele's brand uses a **hero-integrated email capture** for RENACER —
NOT an aggressive popup. The capture lives above the fold inside the
homepage hero section as a single primary action:

- Small gold hairline + eyebrow: `RENACER™ · Guía Editorial Gratuita`
- Italic serif sub-lede: the RENACER subtitle (above)
- Single-row form: email input (underline style) + burgundy outline button
- Trust line beneath: `Una guía editorial · Sin spam · Solo Renacer.`
- Form action lives in `RENACER_FORM_ACTION` inside `hero.tsx` — update
  this constant with the real ConvertKit form ID before launch.

A **subtle secondary popup** may be added later — exit-intent only, never
for already-subscribed visitors, never aggressive. This is documented in
the strategy doc but not yet built.

## Premium vocabulary Dr. Gissele prefers
- **"Códigos Exclusivos"** — instead of generic "letters" or "content"
- **"Próximo Nivel"** — instead of generic "next chapter" or "growth"
- **"Linaje"** — instead of "record" or "registry" when referring to
  honors / credentials
- **"Despertar"** — instead of "becoming" when describing the inner
  journey (especially in the essays / insights context)
- **"Transformación"** (capitalized as a proper noun) — instead of
  generic "change" or "growth"
- **"Inner Circle"** — kept in English even on Spanish site; refers to
  the close community of Despierta Tu Poder participants

## Voice rules
- First-person sparingly, mostly third-person editorial
- Specific over general (Bogotá > "Latin America"; Jeshua > "my son";
  Les Brown > "a famous speaker") because specifics create authority
- Spiritually grounded without being denominational; Christian Leadership
  is in her degree, weave in "calling," "purpose," "becoming"
- No hustle vocabulary, no business-coach jargon
- Audience-broadening: prefer "people" / "those" over "women" in general
  brand voice; reserve "women" for the Despierta Tu Poder Experience
- **Never use "cohort" / "cohorte"** because it feels too corporate. Use
  "Experience" / "Experiencia" for the program/edition, and
  "Inner Circle" for the community of participants.
- **Never use em-dash separators ("—") in body copy.** Dr. Gissele finds
  them less refined than the rest of the editorial system. Use instead:
  colons (":") for "introduction → definition," semicolons (";") for
  joining related thoughts, commas for parentheticals, middle dot ("·")
  for visual list separators in eyebrows and credentials, and periods
  with a new sentence whenever it reads cleaner. Em-dashes are still
  fine inside developer comments — only the user-facing copy is
  affected by this rule.

# Technical Architecture & Editing

## Stack
- Next.js 16 (App Router) on Vercel
- React 19
- Tailwind CSS v4
- Motion (Framer Motion)
- Edge-cached static rendering by default

## Architecture principles
- Lightweight and low-maintenance for long-term ownership
- Vercel-optimized (server-rendered HTML, edge caching)
- Client components only where necessary: Nav, Reveal, Newsletter form
- Prioritize clarity, speed, SEO, and responsiveness over excessive
  animations
- Keep transitions subtle and premium

## Editing surface — every editable thing lives in one of these files
- **`src/lib/i18n/es.ts`** — every Spanish string on the site. Primary
  editing surface for Dr. Gissele since the default site language is
  Spanish.
- **`src/lib/i18n/en.ts`** — every English string. Must stay structurally
  in sync with es.ts (TypeScript enforces this via the shared Dictionary
  type in `src/lib/i18n/types.ts`).
- `src/lib/site.ts` — URLs, Stan Store handle, emails, credentials,
  digital products, press assets, social. The CTAs across the site
  inherit from `LINKS` here. The biographies live as `BIOS` in this
  file (used by /press and JSON-LD).
- `src/lib/media.ts` — every image reference (hero portraits, logos,
  section imagery, gallery photos, founders, community).
- `src/lib/essays.ts` — all writing (Insights). Each essay nests `es`
  and `en` variants.
- `src/components/sections/witness.tsx` — testimonial copy (anonymous).
- `src/components/sections/newsletter.tsx` — ConvertKit form action URL.
- `src/app/[locale]/privacy/page.tsx` & `terms/page.tsx` — legal copy.

## Recognition section — full anatomy

The homepage Recognition section has THREE visual moments stacked:

1. **Editorial masthead photo** at the top (currently `recognition-phd.jpg`
   via `RECOGNITION_PRIMARY`) — full-width 21:9 frame with gold inset,
   gradient burgundy wash, masthead label, conferral caption.

2. **Three honor cards** (Doctorate · Presidential · Senate) — each
   carries its lead photo via `HONOR_PHOTOS[i]`, set in a 3:4 portrait
   frame with the signature gold inset, plus Roman numeral, year,
   gold dot rule, honor title, and "Otorgado Por" + conferring body.
   Photos map by index to `honors.titles` in the dictionary.

3. **"Momentos del Linaje" gallery** beneath the three cards —
   three-column editorial strip with a centered eyebrow + italic
   serif title above. Each column is anchored by a small gold
   eyebrow naming the event, then TWO stacked 4:5 photos with gold
   insets and serif-italic captions. Photos come from `HONOR_GALLERY`
   in `src/lib/media.ts`, captions live in `recognitionHome.galleryCaptions`
   in the i18n dictionaries (indexed by column 0 = PhD, 1 =
   Presidential, 2 = Senate).

To add MORE photos per honor (e.g. `honor-phd-04.jpg`), add a new
entry to `HONOR_GALLERY[event]` and extend the column to render it.

## Honor photo naming convention
For each honor event, photos follow this pattern:
- `honor-{event}.jpg` — the lead portrait (the homepage card photo)
- `honor-{event}-02.jpg`, `honor-{event}-03.jpg`, … — supporting
  moments used in the Momentos gallery and elsewhere
